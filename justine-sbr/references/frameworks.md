# Strategic Frameworks Reference

## G.R.O.W. Framework

The primary diagnostic framework for Strategic Business Reviews. Use as a one-page conversation structure.

### Goal
State explicit and unspoken goals. Common unspoken goals:
- **Freedom** — "I want it to run without me"
- **Consistency** — Predictable income, fewer surprises
- **Legacy** — Something that outlasts the founder
- **Health** — Business that doesn't consume wellbeing

Mirror the owner's words:
> "You said, 'I want it to run without me.' That's not laziness — that's leadership maturity."

### Reality
Diagnose with objectivity but warmth. Identify:
- What's true now
- What the data hides
- What the dashboard can't see

> "Profit is healthy, but time is thin. The system runs, but it doesn't breathe yet."

Key reality questions:
- What moved? Why?
- What is the one change needed?
- Where is the business relying on memory, energy, or personal pushing instead of systems?

### Options
Offer 3–5 paths forward mixing:
- **Strategic levers** — Price, mix, channels, partnerships
- **Leadership habits** — Delegation, review rhythms, calendar protection

> "You don't need more ideas. You need one lever pulled rhythmically."

Example options:
- Price integrity (courage to charge properly)
- Rhythm of review (weekly cash, monthly P&L)
- Delegation confidence (documented processes)
- Client flow structure (moments of truth)
- Deal structure clarity (staged payments)

### Way Forward
Summarise 90-day plan with numbered priorities using verbs:

**Action Box — 90-Day Focus**
1. Map one weekly financial rhythm.
2. Define two KPIs that actually change behaviour.
3. Book time off the tools — leadership by design.

End with reflection cue:
> "Clarity isn't complexity — it's rhythm made visible."

## Business Lifecycle Stages

Determine stage to match tools and advice appropriately. Using wrong tools at wrong stage wastes energy.

### Start-Up Stage
**Needs:** Oxygen (cash) and scrappy selling
**Symptoms:**
- Still proving model
- Cash is the constraint
- Founder does everything
- No documented processes

**Appropriate interventions:**
- Focus on first paying customers
- Simple cash tracking (weekly)
- "Fast yes" offers
- Ruthless prioritisation

### Growth Stage
**Needs:** Documentation and repeatability
**Symptoms:**
- Revenue growing but chaos increasing
- Hiring but roles unclear
- Founder becoming bottleneck
- Systems exist but aren't followed

**Appropriate interventions:**
- Document core processes (one page each)
- Define role outcomes, not just duties
- Weekly team rhythm
- Pipeline stages and qualification rules

### Maturity Stage
**Needs:** Small steady improvements
**Symptoms:**
- Stable revenue but flat growth
- Systems work but aren't optimised
- Team capable but coasting
- Margins could improve

**Appropriate interventions:**
- Continuous improvement rhythms
- Margin analysis by product/service
- Cross-training for resilience
- Experiment portfolio (3–5 running)

### Stagnation Stage
**Needs:** Truth-telling
**Symptoms:**
- Flat or declining metrics
- "We've always done it this way"
- Avoidance of hard conversations
- Founder fatigue

**Appropriate interventions:**
- Honest numbers review
- "Zombie products" retirement
- Values and standards reset
- Courage lever identification

### Decline Stage
**Needs:** Decisions
**Symptoms:**
- Consistent losses
- Key people leaving
- Customer complaints rising
- Cash crisis

**Appropriate interventions:**
- Triage priorities ruthlessly
- Cash runway calculation
- Turnaround or exit planning
- Protected decision-making time

### Renewal / Exit Stage
**Needs:** Plan and calm head
**Symptoms:**
- Ready to sell or transition
- Business depends on founder
- No documentation of value
- Unclear succession path

**Appropriate interventions:**
- "Function-without-founder" sprints
- Clean business artifacts
- Sellability assessment
- 12-month transition roadmap

## Business Vitals Dashboard

### Core Vitals (Review Weekly, 20 mins)
Pick 5 maximum:
- **Leads** — Qualified leads per week
- **Show-ups** — Appointments kept
- **Conversion** — Proposals to wins
- **Average order value** — Revenue per transaction
- **Time from order to cash** — Days to payment

### Quality Checks
- **First-pass success** — Right first time %
- **Rework rate** — Jobs requiring correction

### Capacity Checks
- **Utilisation** — Productive time / available time
- **On-time delivery** — Promises kept %

### Financial Vitals
- **Gross margin** — Revenue minus direct costs
- **Cash runway** — Weeks of cash available
- **Receivables age** — Days to collect

## Pipeline Math Structure

Simple one-page view for weekly review:
1. Leads contacted
2. Leads qualified
3. First meetings booked
4. Proposals issued
5. Deals won
6. **Cash collected** (not just contracted)

Emphasise: "Separate 'pipeline value' from 'cash collected' in your weekly review."

## Cash Rhythm Framework

### Three Rhythms That Calm the Nervous System

1. **Daily** — 5-minute cash awareness (what's in, what's out today)
2. **Weekly** — 13-week cash forecast (one line per week)
3. **Monthly** — 45-minute P&L review with decisions

### Receivables Cadence
- Day 0: Invoice on completion
- Day 7: First follow-up call
- Day 14: Second call
- Day 21: Escalation

### Staged Commercial Model
For businesses with long-cycle or back-ended revenue:

**Stage 1:** Paid engagement + short-cycle revenue (oxygen)
**Stage 2:** Larger delivery with milestone invoicing (stability)
**Stage 3:** Long-term wealth outcomes (legacy)

> "If you skip Stage 1, you will keep returning to cashflow pain."

## Moments of Truth Framework

Identify and standardise the few touchpoints that shape client trust:

1. **First reply** — Speed and professionalism
2. **First call** — Structure and context-setting
3. **Proposal clarity** — Problem, outcome, scope, timeline, investment, risks
4. **How you handle a delay** — Tell them before they ask
5. **How you confirm next steps** — No call ends without a date/time
6. **How money is discussed** — Transparent terms

Script the **desired feeling** for each moment (relief, certainty, pride) and the behaviours that create it.

## Function Framework

Start with functions, not faces. Every business has these core functions:

1. **Leadership** — Strategy, decisions, culture
2. **Finance** — Cash, margins, forecasting
3. **Marketing** — Awareness, positioning, content
4. **Sales** — Pipeline, conversion, proposals
5. **Operations/Production** — Delivery, quality, throughput
6. **People/HR** — Hiring, training, standards
7. **Admin/IT** — Systems, tools, infrastructure

For each function define:
- **Purpose** — Why it exists
- **Key numbers** — What to measure
- **Core processes** — Few critical workflows

Place names in boxes. Founder's name will appear in many boxes at first — make this visible so relief can be designed.

## Experiment Template

For continuous improvement:

| Element | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| Hypothesis | If we [change X], then [metric Y] will [improve by Z] |
| Metric | Specific, measurable outcome |
| Time-box | Duration of experiment (typically 1-2 weeks) |
| Owner | Single accountable person |
| Decision | Keep, tweak, or kill |

Maintain 3–5 experiments running at any time. Track on simple board: To Try → Running → Reviewing → Done.

## 90-Day Planning Structure

**Quarter Theme:** Single rallying cry (e.g., "Every Dollar Counts")

**Per Function:**
- One metric to improve
- Three weekly actions
- One owner
- Review day/time

**Weekly rhythm:**
- 30-minute review: What moved? Why? What's the one change?

**Mid-quarter reset:**
- Stop / Start / Continue (two items per column)

**Quarter-end memo:**
- Results, lessons, changes for next quarter
